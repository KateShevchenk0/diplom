# Ukrainian myths

Ukrainian myths are a set of imaginary, folklore stories and legends that are of great importance in Ukrainian culture and traditions. They are passed down from generation to generation and tell about the origin of the world, natural phenomena, the history of the people, as well as mythological creatures, heroes and deities.
Ukrainian myths often have their roots in ancient practices and beliefs related to nature and the cosmos. They reflect ideas about the distribution of roles between gods, heroes and humans, as well as the distinction between good and evil. Many of these myths are related to agriculture, natural phenomena, and rituals that occur during various festivals and ceremonies.
Ukrainian myths can be found in folk tales, legends, songs and ancient works. They reflect the richness of Ukrainian culture, its specificity and peculiarities. These myths are an important part of the national heritage of Ukraine and serve as important symbols of national self-awareness and identity.
