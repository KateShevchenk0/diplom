# Kyivo-Pecherska Lavra

Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra is an outstanding monument of Ukraine, which occupies a special place in the history, culture and religious life of the country. Located in Kyiv, this spiritual fortress has the status of a national reserve and is included in the UNESCO world heritage list.
The Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra was founded in the 11th century and became one of the most important monasteries in Eastern Europe. It consists of a complex of church buildings, a cave monastery, museum exhibits and other historical objects.
The central part of the Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra is the Dormition Cathedral, which served as the main temple of the monastery. It impresses with its architecture and is decorated with rich frescoes and icons. The Lavra also includes the St. Michael's Gold-Top Monastery, which is a symbol of Kyiv, as well as other church buildings, caves, and museum collections.
